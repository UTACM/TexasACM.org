About Us

The Association for Computing Machinery is a UT chapter of the national ACM, and exists to create a community among Computer Science students and anyone interested in technology. ACM holds events that serve its members and the community, and is focused on the advancement of computer science studies and research, furthering computing as a profession and a science.

Why Join?

Academic

Our mentorship program matches upperclassmen with incoming freshman, who can help out with the social, academic, and career aspects of college. The mentorship program aims to form tight-knit friends and connections. You can apply to be a mentor or mentee here.

Network / Professional

Professional networking opportunities are available to all ACM members. Our General Meetings and Tech Talks allow members to meet company recruiters, improve their résumés, and ultimately get interviews.

Social

Don't just work all the time; fun and friends are important too! Make friends by joining ACM, and have fun at our many social events such as Family Dinners, Intramural Sports, Retreat, and Gaming Nights!

Programming Competitions

Our programming competitions are a fun way to challenge your coding abilities with interview-like questions. Not a coding wizard yet? Join us for workshops to learn! All experience levels are welcome!

ACM Office

Located at GDC 3.312, the ACM office offers a wide variety of amenities including comfy couches, a TV with video games, free coffee, 75¢ sodas, board games, and textbooks.

Leadership Opportunities

Boost your résumé by being a leader in the largest CS org on campus. Apply to be an Operational Officer in the Fall and work directly with a Senior Officer on important tasks to keep the org running smoothly.
